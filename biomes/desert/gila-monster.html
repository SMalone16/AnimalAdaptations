Desert

Slow and steady hunter

Gila monsters move slowly under desert shrubs. They spend most of their lives in burrows to avoid the heat.

Venom Bite

Grooves in their teeth deliver venom that helps them catch and hold prey.

Fat Storage

A thick tail stores fat, letting the lizard go months between meals.

Shady Lifestyle

Spending days underground keeps them cool and safe from predators.
