Savannah

Ready to charge

Warthogs trot with their tails held high. They use their tusks and snouts to dig for roots and to defend their burrows.

Protective Tusks

Sharp tusks help warthogs defend themselves from predators.

Kneeling Knees

Warthogs bend on their front knees to dig and eat, using padded calluses for comfort.

Burrow Backups

They borrow empty aardvark burrows for shelter and back in so their tusks face out.

Bravery and burrows keep warthogs safe.
