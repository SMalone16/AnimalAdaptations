Savannah

Speed built-in

Cheetahs sprint across open plains chasing gazelles. Their slim bodies are designed for speed over short bursts.

Lightweight Frame

A small head, long legs, and flexible spine let cheetahs stretch and spring forward like rubber bands.

Grip Claws

Semi-retractable claws work like track shoes, digging into the ground for extra grip.

Tear Marks

Dark lines under their eyes act like sunglasses, blocking glare so they can focus on running prey.

Cheetahs are built from nose to tail for quick dashes.
